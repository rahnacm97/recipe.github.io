Biriyani

Ingredients:

    For the Chicken Marinade:

  • 500g chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Salt to taste

    • For the Rice:

  • 2 cups Jeerakasala rice (or basmati rice if not available)
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 4-5 cloves
  • 2-3 cardamom pods
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 teaspoon ghee
  • Salt to taste

    • For the Biryani:

  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1/2 cup chopped mint leaves
  • 1/2 cup chopped coriander leaves
  • 1 teaspoon fennel seeds
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 2 tablespoons ghee
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1/4 cup raisins
  • 1/4 cup cashew nuts
  • 1/4 cup fried onions (optional, for garnish)
  • 2 boiled eggs (optional, for garnish)
  • A few saffron strands soaked in 2 tablespoons of warm milk (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the Chicken:
    • In a large bowl, mix the chicken pieces with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, lemon juice, and salt. Marinate for at least 1 hour, or overnight for better flavor.
  2. Cook the Rice:
    • Wash the Jeerakasala rice and soak it for 30 minutes.
    • In a large pot, bring water to a boil. Add bay leaf, cloves, cardamom pods, cinnamon stick, ghee, and salt.
    • Add the soaked and drained rice to the boiling water. Cook until the rice is 70-80% done. Drain and set aside.
  3. Prepare the Chicken Masala:
    • Heat oil and ghee in a large, heavy-bottomed pan.
    • Add fennel seeds and let them splutter. Add the sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.
    • Add green chilies, chopped tomatoes, and salt. Cook until the tomatoes are soft.
    • Add the marinated chicken and cook until the chicken is almost done.
    • Add garam masala, turmeric powder, red chili powder, cumin powder, mint leaves, and coriander leaves. Mix well and cook for a few more minutes.
  4. Fry the Cashews and Raisins:
    • In a small pan, heat a little ghee and fry the cashew nuts until golden. Remove and set aside.
    • In the same pan, fry the raisins until they puff up. Remove and set aside.
  5. Layering the Biryani:
    • In the pot with the chicken masala, spread half of the partially cooked rice over the chicken layer.
    • Drizzle half of the saffron milk over the rice (if using). Add a layer of the remaining rice on top.
    • Drizzle the remaining saffron milk over the top layer of rice. Sprinkle the fried cashews, raisins, and fried onions on top.
  6. Dum Cooking:
    • Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid or seal it with aluminum foil to trap the steam.
    • Cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  7. Serve:
    • Gently fluff the biryani with a fork before serving. Garnish with boiled eggs if desired.
    • Serve your delicious Kerala chicken biryani with raita, pickle, and pappadam.

Tips

  • Jeerakasala rice is traditional for Kerala biryani, but basmati rice can be used as a substitute.
  • Adding fried onions and nuts enhances the flavor and texture of the biryani.
  • Serve with cucumber raita and a squeeze of lemon juice for a refreshing touch.

Avial

Ingredients:

    Vegetables (choose 4-5 types):

    • 1 carrot, peeled and cut into 2-inch sticks
    • 1 potato, peeled and cut into 2-inch sticks
    • 1 raw banana (plantain), peeled and cut into 2-inch sticks
    • 1/2 cup drumsticks (moringa), cut into 2-inch pieces
    • 1/2 cup ash gourd, peeled and cut into 2-inch sticks
    • 1 cucumber, peeled and cut into 2-inch sticks
    • 5-6 green beans, cut into 2-inch pieces
    • 1 small piece of yam, peeled and cut into 2-inch sticks
    • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1 cup grated coconut
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2 tablespoons plain yogurt (optional, for a sour taste)
  • 1 tablespoon coconut oil
  • 2 sprigs curry leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed

Instructions:

  1. Make the Zucchini Noodles:
    • Wash, peel, and cut all the vegetables into uniform 2-inch sticks. This ensures even cooking.
  2. Prepare the Pesto:
    • In a food processor, combine basil leaves, pine nuts, nutritional yeast, garlic, salt, and pepper. While processing, slowly add olive oil until the mixture is smooth and creamy.
  3. Combine:
    • Toss the zucchini noodles with the pesto sauce until well-coated.
  4. Serve:
    • Garnish with cherry tomatoes and serve immediately. You can enjoy this dish raw or lightly sauté the zoodles in a pan for a warm dish.
