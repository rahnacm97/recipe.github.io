Stew

Ingredients:

  • 250g chicken or mixed vegetables (carrot, potato, beans)
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1-inch piece ginger, julienned
  • 1 cup thick coconut milk
  • 1 cup thin coconut milk
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper powder
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat coconut oil in a pot and sauté the onions, green chilies, and ginger until the onions turn translucent.
  2. Add the chicken or vegetables, salt, and thin coconut milk. Cover and cook until the chicken is cooked or the vegetables are tender.
  3. Add the thick coconut milk and pepper powder. Simmer for a few minutes without boiling.
  4. Garnish with curry leaves and serve hot with dishes.

Beef Fry

Ingredients:

  • 500g beef, cut into small pieces
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tablespoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 tablespoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper powder
  • 1 tablespoon vinegar
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the beef with red chili powder, turmeric powder, coriander powder, salt, and vinegar. Set aside for 30 minutes.
  2. Pressure cook the marinated beef with a little water until tender.
  3. In a large pan, heat coconut oil and sauté the onions, green chilies, ginger-garlic paste, and curry leaves until the onions turn golden brown.
  4. Add the cooked beef along with black pepper powder. Fry on medium heat until the beef is dark and crispy.
  5. Serve hot with dishes.

Kadala Curry

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup black chickpeas (kadala), soaked overnight
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 tablespoon red chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 cup coconut milk (optional)
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Pressure cook the soaked black chickpeas with turmeric powder and salt until tender.
  2. Heat coconut oil in a pan and sauté the onions, green chilies, and ginger-garlic paste until the onions turn golden brown.
  3. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until soft. Add red chili powder, coriander powder, and garam masala. Cook for a minute.
  4. Add the cooked chickpeas along with the cooking water and simmer for 10 minutes.
  5. Add coconut milk (if using) and simmer for a few more minutes. Garnish with curry leaves.
  6. Serve hot with dishes.

Vegetable Kurma

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (carrot, potato, peas, beans, cauliflower)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 tablespoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 cup coconut, grated
  • 1 tablespoon poppy seeds (optional)
  • 1/4 cup cashew nuts (optional)
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Grind the grated coconut, poppy seeds, and cashew nuts into a smooth paste with a little water. Set aside.
  2. Heat coconut oil in a pot and sauté the onions, green chilies, and ginger-garlic paste until the onions turn golden brown.
  3. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until soft. Add turmeric powder, coriander powder, and garam masala. Cook for a minute.
  4. Add the mixed vegetables and salt. Pour in enough water to cover the vegetables and cook until tender.
  5. Add the ground coconut paste and simmer for 5-10 minutes. Garnish with curry leaves.
  6. Serve hot with dishes.

Chapathi

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour (atta)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon oil or ghee (optional)
  • Water as needed

Instructions:

  1. Mix the Dough:
    • In a large mixing bowl, add the whole wheat flour and salt. Mix well.
    • Gradually add water little by little, and mix the flour with your hand to form a soft dough.
    • Add the oil or ghee (if using) and knead the dough for about 5-10 minutes until it's smooth and pliable. The dough should be soft but not sticky.
    • Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for at least 20-30 minutes. Resting the dough helps in making soft chapathis.
  2. Roll the Chapathis:
    • After resting, divide the dough into small lemon-sized balls.
    • Lightly flour a clean surface and take one dough ball. Flatten it slightly with your fingers and roll it out using a rolling pin into a round shape, about 6-7 inches in diameter. The rolled chapathi should be thin but not too thin to tear.
  3. Cook the Chapathis:
    • Heat a tawa or flat griddle on medium-high heat.
    • Place the rolled chapathi on the hot tawa. Cook for about 30 seconds until you see small bubbles forming on the surface.
    • Flip the chapathi and cook the other side for another 30 seconds.
    • Press down the edges of the chapathi with a clean cloth or spatula to help it puff up. Flip again and cook for a few more seconds until both sides have light brown spots.
    • Remove the chapathi from the tawa and brush it with ghee or butter, if desired.
  4. Serve:
    • Serve the chapathis hot with your favorite curry, dal, or yogurt. They pair especially well with Kerala-style chicken curry, vegetable kurma, or any gravy dish.

    Tips

    • Kneading the dough well and letting it rest are key to making soft chapathis.
    • Cook the chapathis on medium-high heat to prevent them from becoming hard.
    • Keep the cooked chapathis in a covered container to keep them soft until serving.

Kerala Chicken Curry (Nadan Kozhi Curry)

Ingredients:

    For Marination:

  • 500g chicken, cut into medium-sized pieces
  • 1 tablespoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • Salt to taste

    • For the Curry:

  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1-inch piece ginger, chopped
  • 5-6 garlic cloves, chopped
  • 2 medium tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 tablespoon coriander powder
  • 1 tablespoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 cup thick coconut milk
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed

    • For Tempering:

  • 1 tablespoon coconut oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 dried red chili
  • 1 sprig curry leaves

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the Chicken:
    • In a bowl, marinate the chicken pieces with red chili powder, turmeric powder, ginger-garlic paste, and salt. Set aside for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Prepare the Curry:
    • Heat coconut oil in a large pan or kadai. Add the sliced onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
    • Add the green chilies, julienned ginger, and chopped garlic. Sauté for a few minutes until the raw smell disappears.
    • Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften and blend with the onions.
    • Add turmeric powder, coriander powder, red chili powder, and black pepper powder. Fry the spices for a minute.
    • Add the marinated chicken pieces to the pan and mix well with the spice mixture. Cook for about 5-7 minutes, allowing the chicken to brown slightly.
    • Add enough water to cover the chicken, bring it to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer until the chicken is cooked through and tender (about 20-25 minutes).
    • Once the chicken is cooked, add the thick coconut milk and garam masala. Stir well and simmer for another 5 minutes. Do not boil after adding the coconut milk.
    • Check the seasoning and add salt if needed. Add the curry leaves and stir well.
  3. Tempering:
    • In a small pan, heat coconut oil and add mustard seeds. When they splutter, add dried red chili and curry leaves.
    • Pour this tempering over the chicken curry and mix well.
  4. Serve:
    • Serve the Kerala chicken curry hot with steamed rice, Kerala parotta, appam, or chapathi.
