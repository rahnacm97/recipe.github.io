Puttu

Ingredients:

  • Rice flour (Puttu podi) – 2 cups
  • Grated coconut – 1 cup
  • Water – 1 cup (approx.)
  • Salt – to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Rice Flour:
    • Take the rice flour in a large mixing bowl.
    • Add a pinch of salt to the flour.
    • Gradually sprinkle water into the flour and mix with your hands. The flour should be moist but not form a dough. The consistency should be crumbly, like wet sand. To check, take a little flour and press it; it should hold its shape and then crumble when you touch it.
  2. Layering the Puttu:
    • Use a puttu maker (cylindrical steamer) for this step. If you don’t have one, you can use a coconut shell or any small steamer.
    • Start by adding a thin layer of grated coconut at the bottom of the puttu maker.
    • Then, add a layer of the moistened rice flour on top of the coconut. Don’t press it down; just let it fall in loosely.
    • Add another layer of grated coconut, followed by the rice flour. Repeat this process until the puttu maker is full, finishing with a layer of grated coconut
  3. Steaming:
    • Assemble the puttu maker and attach it to the pressure cooker or a pot with boiling water.
    • Steam the puttu for about 5-7 minutes until the steam starts coming out of the top, and the rice flour is cooked.
    • Once done, carefully remove the puttu from the maker using the stick provided. Push it out onto a plate.
  4. Serving:
    • Serve hot with kadala curry (black chickpeas curry), banana, or any side dish of your choice.

Idli

Ingredients:

  • Parboiled rice – 2 cups
  • Urad dal (split black gram) – 1 cup
  • Fenugreek seeds (methi) – 1 teaspoon
  • Salt – to taste
  • Water – as needed
  • Oil or ghee – for greasing the idli molds

Instructions:

  1. Soaking:
    • Wash the rice and urad dal separately in water several times.
    • Soak the rice in a bowl with enough water for at least 4-6 hours.
    • Soak the urad dal and fenugreek seeds together in another bowl with enough water for at least 4-6 hours.
  2. Grinding:
    • Drain the water from the soaked urad dal and fenugreek seeds.
    • Grind the urad dal and fenugreek seeds in a grinder or blender, adding water gradually, until you get a smooth and fluffy batter. Transfer this to a large bowl.
    • Next, drain the rice and grind it to a slightly coarse batter, adding water as needed. The rice batter should be slightly grainy but not too coarse. Mix the rice batter with the urad dal batter.
  3. Fermentation:
    • Add salt to the batter and mix well. The batter should be of pouring consistency but thick enough to coat the back of a spoon.
    • Cover the bowl with a lid and let it ferment in a warm place for 8-12 hours or overnight. The batter will double in size during fermentation.
  4. Steaming:
    • Once the batter is well-fermented, mix it gently.
    • Grease the idli molds with a little oil or ghee.
    • Pour the batter into the molds, filling them about ¾ full.
    • Heat water in an idli steamer or pressure cooker (without the whistle) and place the idli stand inside.
    • Steam the idlis on medium heat for 10-12 minutes. Check for doneness by inserting a toothpick or knife; it should come out clean.
  5. Serving:
    • Remove the idli stand from the steamer and let it cool for a few minutes.
    • Use a spoon to carefully remove the idlis from the molds.
    • Serve hot with sambar, coconut chutney, or any side dish of your choice.

Dosa

Ingredients:

  • Parboiled rice – 2 cups
  • Urad dal (split black gram) – ½ cup
  • Fenugreek seeds (methi) – ½ teaspoon
  • Chana dal (split chickpeas) – 2 tablespoons (optional)
  • Salt – to taste
  • Water – as needed
  • Oil or ghee – for cooking

Instructions:

  1. Soaking:
    • Wash the rice and dals (urad dal and chana dal) separately in water several times.
    • Soak the rice and fenugreek seeds together in a bowl with enough water for at least 4-6 hours.
    • Soak the urad dal and chana dal together in another bowl with enough water for at least 4-6 hours.
  2. Grinding:
    • Drain the water from the soaked urad dal and chana dal.
    • Grind the urad dal and chana dal in a grinder or blender, adding water gradually, until you get a smooth and fluffy batter. Transfer this to a large bowl.
    • Next, drain the rice and fenugreek seeds and grind them to a smooth batter, adding water as needed. The batter should be smooth with a slight grainy texture. Mix the rice batter with the urad dal batter.
  3. Fermentation:
    • Add salt to the batter and mix well. The batter should have a flowing consistency but not too runny.
    • Cover the bowl with a lid and let it ferment in a warm place for 8-12 hours or overnight. The batter will double in size during fermentation.
  4. Making Dosa:
    • Once the batter is well-fermented, mix it gently. If the batter is too thick, add a little water to adjust the consistency.
    • Heat a non-stick tawa (griddle) or a cast-iron skillet on medium heat. Once hot, lightly grease it with a few drops of oil or ghee.
    • Pour a ladleful of batter onto the center of the tawa and quickly spread it in a circular motion using the back of the ladle to form a thin crepe.
    • Drizzle a few drops of oil or ghee around the edges of the dosa and on top.
    • Cook the dosa on medium heat until the edges start to lift, and the bottom turns golden brown.
    • If you prefer a crispy dosa, cook it for a little longer. For a softer dosa, reduce the cooking time slightly.
    • Once done, fold the dosa in half or roll it and remove it from the tawa.
  5. Serving:
    • Serve hot with coconut chutney, sambar, or any side dish of your choice.
    • Repeat the process with the remaining batter, greasing the tawa lightly between dosas if needed.

Appam

Ingredients:

  • Raw rice – 1 cup
  • Cooked rice – 2 tablespoons
  • Grated coconut – ½ cup
  • Sugar – 1 tablespoon
  • Salt – to taste
  • Instant yeast – ½ teaspoon (or ¼ teaspoon if using active dry yeast)
  • Water – as needed

Instructions:

  1. Soaking:
    • Wash the raw rice thoroughly and soak it in enough water for 4-6 hours.
  2. Grinding:
    • Drain the soaked rice and transfer it to a blender.
    • Add the cooked rice, grated coconut, and a little water to the blender.
    • Blend until you get a smooth batter. The batter should be slightly thick but still of pouring consistency.
    • If using active dry yeast, dissolve it in a few tablespoons of warm water with a pinch of sugar and let it sit for 5-10 minutes until frothy. If using instant yeast, you can add it directly to the batter.
    • Add the yeast and sugar to the batter and mix well.
  3. Fermentation:
    • Cover the batter and let it ferment in a warm place for 8-12 hours or overnight. The batter should rise and become frothy.
  4. Making Appam:
    • Once the batter is fermented, add salt and mix gently. If the batter is too thick, add a little water to adjust the consistency.
    • Heat an appam pan (a small, rounded non-stick pan) on medium heat. Lightly grease the pan with a few drops of oil.
    • Pour a ladleful of batter into the center of the pan. Immediately hold the handles of the pan and swirl it in a circular motion so that the batter spreads out, forming a thin layer around the edges and a slightly thicker center.
    • Cover the pan with a lid and cook on low to medium heat for 2-3 minutes until the appam is cooked through, the edges are crispy, and the center is soft and fluffy.
    • There’s no need to flip the appam; it’s cooked on one side only.
  5. Serving:
    • Carefully remove the appam from the pan and serve hot with coconut milk, stew, or any side dish of your choice.
    • Repeat with the remaining batter.

Masala Dosa

Ingredients:

    For Dosa Batter:

  • Parboiled rice – 2 cups
  • Urad dal (split black gram) – ½ cup
  • Fenugreek seeds (methi) – ½ teaspoon
  • Chana dal (split chickpeas) – 2 tablespoons (optional)
  • Salt – to taste
  • Water – as needed

    • For Masala Filling:

  • Potatoes – 4 medium-sized, boiled and mashed
  • Onion – 1 large, thinly sliced
  • Green chilies – 2-3, chopped
  • Ginger – 1-inch piece, finely chopped
  • Curry leaves – a few
  • Mustard seeds – ½ teaspoon
  • Turmeric powder – ¼ teaspoon
  • Asafoetida (hing) – a pinch
  • Chana dal – 1 tablespoon
  • Urad dal – 1 teaspoon
  • Oil – 2 tablespoons
  • Salt – to taste
  • Coriander leaves – for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preparing the Dosa Batter:
    • Wash the rice, urad dal, and chana dal separately. Soak the rice and fenugreek seeds together in water for 4-6 hours. Soak the urad dal and chana dal in another bowl of water for 4-6 hours.
    • Drain the water from the soaked urad dal and chana dal. Grind them together to a smooth and fluffy batter, adding water as needed. Transfer to a large bowl.
    • Drain and grind the rice and fenugreek seeds to a smooth batter with a slightly coarse texture. Mix this batter with the urad dal batter.
    • Add salt and mix well. Cover the batter and let it ferment overnight or for 8-12 hours until it doubles in volume.
  2. Making the Masala Filling:
    • Heat oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
    • Add urad dal, chana dal, and asafoetida, and sauté until the dals turn golden brown.
    • Add curry leaves, chopped green chilies, and ginger, and sauté for a minute.
    • Add sliced onions and sauté until they turn golden.
    • Add turmeric powder and mix well.
    • Add the mashed potatoes, salt to taste, and mix everything together. Cook for a few minutes to allow the flavors to blend.
    • Garnish with chopped coriander leaves if desired. Set the masala aside.
  3. Making the Dosa:
    • Heat a non-stick tawa or cast-iron skillet on medium heat. Lightly grease it with oil.
    • Pour a ladleful of batter onto the center of the tawa and spread it out in a circular motion to form a thin crepe.
    • Drizzle a few drops of oil around the edges of the dosa.
    • Cook until the dosa turns golden and crispy on the edges.
    • Place a portion of the prepared potato masala in the center of the dosa.
    • Fold the dosa over the masala to form a semi-circle or roll it up.
  4. Serving:
    • Serve the masala dosa hot with coconut chutney, sambar, and a side of your choice.

Upma

Ingredients:

  • Semolina (rava/suji) – 1 cup
  • Onion – 1 medium, finely chopped
  • Green chilies – 2, slit
  • Ginger – 1 teaspoon, finely chopped
  • Curry leaves – a few
  • Mustard seeds – ½ teaspoon
  • Urad dal (split black gram) – 1 teaspoon
  • Chana dal (split chickpeas) – 1 teaspoon
  • Cashew nuts – 8-10 (optional)
  • Water – 2½ cups
  • Ghee or oil – 2 tablespoons
  • Salt – to taste
  • Coriander leaves – for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Roasting the Semolina:
    • Heat a pan on medium heat and dry roast the semolina until it turns light golden and gives off a nutty aroma. Stir continuously to avoid burning.
    • Once roasted, transfer the semolina to a plate and set it aside.
  2. Tempering:
    • In the same pan, heat ghee or oil on medium heat.
    • Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
    • Add urad dal, chana dal, and cashew nuts, and sauté until they turn golden brown.
    • Add curry leaves, green chilies, and chopped ginger. Sauté for a minute until the ginger is aromatic.
    • Add the chopped onions and sauté until they become translucent.
  3. Cooking the Upma:
    • Pour 2½ cups of water into the pan, add salt to taste, and bring the water to a boil.
    • Once the water starts boiling, gradually add the roasted semolina while stirring continuously to prevent lumps from forming.
    • Lower the heat and cook the upma until the semolina absorbs all the water and becomes soft and fluffy. This should take about 3-4 minutes.
    • Cover the pan and let the upma steam on low heat for a minute or two.
  4. Serving:
    • Fluff the upma with a fork, garnish with chopped coriander leaves if desired, and serve hot.
    • Upma is traditionally served with coconut chutney, pickle, or a side of your choice.

Idiyappam

Ingredients:

  • Rice flour – 2 cups
  • Water – 2 cups
  • Salt – to taste
  • Oil – 1 teaspoon
  • Grated coconut – for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preparing the Dough:
    • Boil 2 cups of water in a saucepan. Add salt and oil to the boiling water.
    • Gradually add the rice flour to the boiling water, stirring continuously to prevent lumps from forming.
    • Once all the rice flour is added, remove the pan from the heat and mix well until it forms a smooth dough.
    • Let the dough cool slightly until it’s warm enough to handle. Knead the dough for a minute or two to make it smooth and pliable.
  2. Making Idiyappam:
    • Grease the idiyappam press (a type of noodle or sev maker) with a little oil.
    • Fill the press with a portion of the dough.
    • Press the dough directly onto greased idli plates, small circular plates, or banana leaves, forming thin noodles or strings.
    • Optionally, sprinkle a little grated coconut on top of the idiyappam for added flavor.
  3. Steaming:
    • Arrange the idiyappam in a steamer or idli cooker.
    • Steam for about 10 minutes on medium heat or until the idiyappam is cooked through. The idiyappam should be soft and translucent.
  4. Serving:
    • Remove the idiyappam from the steamer and let it cool slightly before serving.
    • Serve hot with coconut milk, stew, vegetable curry, or any side dish of your choice.

Pathiri

Ingredients:

  • Rice flour – 2 cups (fine variety, roasted)
  • Water – 2 cups
  • Salt – to taste
  • Coconut oil – 1 teaspoon (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preparing the Dough:
    • Boil 2 cups of water in a saucepan. Add salt and coconut oil (if using).
    • Gradually add the rice flour to the boiling water, stirring continuously to avoid lumps.
    • Once the flour is added, remove the saucepan from heat and mix the dough until it comes together.
    • Let the dough cool slightly until it is warm enough to handle. Then, knead the dough well until it becomes smooth and pliable.
  2. Rolling the Pathiri:
    • Divide the dough into small, equal-sized balls.
    • Dust a clean surface with a little rice flour. Take a dough ball and roll it out into a thin, round disc using a rolling pin. The pathiri should be very thin, about 2-3 mm in thickness.
    • Alternatively, you can press the dough ball between two sheets of plastic or parchment paper using a flat plate or a pathiri press.
  3. Cooking the Pathiri:
    • Heat a tawa (flat griddle) on medium heat.
    • Place the rolled pathiri on the hot tawa. Cook for a few seconds until bubbles start to form on the surface.
    • Flip the pathiri and cook the other side. The pathiri should puff up slightly, and you can gently press the edges with a spatula to help it puff.
    • Cook until both sides have light golden spots and are fully cooked. Do not overcook, as pathiris are meant to be soft.
  4. Serving:
    • Remove the pathiri from the tawa and place it in a container with a lid to keep it soft and warm.
    • Serve hot with a side dish like chicken curry, mutton curry, or vegetable stew. Pathiri pairs especially well with spicy curries.
